When you think about your genetics …. what comes to mind?

Probably traits passed down from your parents .. like your eye and hair color, the shape of your nose, maybe even your hot temper, and of course, the shape of your body.

You have probably heard someone say something similar to this (and possibly even said it yourself) …“Everyone in my family is overweight. It doesn’t matter what I do, it’s my genetics.”

Yes, genetics can determine where you store fat, how much muscle you can pack on, how wide your hips are, and even how your body responds to certain foods. We even preach the importance of training and eating for your specific body type because our FIXED genes can influence many aspects of who we are.

We can’t control the genes we’ve been given. But when it comes to how well you can drop body fat… the finger may need to be pointed at a second set of genes you didn’t get from your parents.

The Second Genome
There are over 37 trillion “human” cells in our bodies that contain our DNA, and the majority of these cells ARE NOT hereditary genes.

It’s a “second set of genes” that researchers call our second genome. Unlike your inherited genes, which are more or less fixed, this second set of genes is not.

57% of the cells that make YOU who YOU ARE and live in your body are not passed down by your parents.

THIS IS GOOD NEWS … we will tell you why and how you can reverse this a little further down in this article.

First, let’s explain what this “second genome” is.

This second set of genes is a bunch of bacteria, fungi, parasites, and viruses! That’s right, you are mostly made up of a bunch of .. well .. bugs.

These microscopic organisms (called MICROBIOME) play a vital role in supporting the daily operation of your body.

You name it… your microbiome helps it function.

  • Food Digestion
  • Nutrient Absorption
  • Energy Production
  • Immune System Regulation (⅔ of our immune system resides in the gut)
  • Skin Health
  • Mental Health/Brain Function
  • ++ MORE

These microbes live everywhere in your body! Bacteria is the most common organism, and the vast majority takes residency in your gut.

A healthy adult will have about FIVE POUNDS of bacteria in their colon. Some are BENEFICIAL… others are HARMFUL!

If you have too much unfriendly bacteria, that is when you may start to have
adverse effects such as…

Here is THE GOOD NEWS …

The organisms live in you today may not be the same as the ones that colonize next week. They are constantly changing and have been since the day you were born.

But this can only happen if you take a very specific and proactive approach to ensure you are swapping out that “bad” bacteria with good bacteria .. or at least maintaining a proper balance of the two.

You need to reach something called “Microbial Harmony.”

What Is Microbial Harmony?
We all have our own “Microbiome Blueprint” that is as unique as your fingerprint … nobody else has YOUR exact microbiome. Even identical twins are made up of a totally different accumulation of microbial life.

Researchers have also found that the organisms that live in people of a healthy weight are VERY DIFFERENT than those that are overweight. This strongly suggests… having a healthy balance of bacteria likely play a crucial role in our ability to lose weight.

The reason our microbiome is so individual (and continually changing) is most likely a result of our personal lifestyle practices as well as our interactions with the world around us.

  • Eating too many processed foods
  • Lack of micro-nutrient diversity
  • Not exercising
  • Environmental toxins (in the air you breathe, the water you drink and bathe in, foods you consume, surfaces you touch, pollution, etc.)
  • Stress
  • Age

As you see… some of these things you do have total control over… others, not so much!
