Important Greens for a Healthy Diet

 

Including Supergreens in Your Meal Plan

We get it. Eating pounds of green fruits and veggies isn’t easy. Here’s some motivation to getting these supergreens into your diet, how they benefit you, and how you can easily add it to your diet without dreading the taste.

Why Supergreens Are Important for Your Fitness Nutrition

Supergreens help your fitness nutrition by promoting weight loss, health, and improving your everyday performance. There are a number of green leafy vegetable benefits to take advantage of.

1. Enhanced Energy

Some greens are great energy boosters. They are low in calories but contain enough carbohydrates to fuel your energy levels. Their magnesium content can also boost your metabolism to quickly turn your carb intake into energy.

2. Cleanse and Detoxify

Most greens are full of antioxidants. Their antioxidants help get rid of the free radicals in your body and aid in the prevention of developing cancer. A green smoothie every 2-3 days is enough to boost your detox diet.

3. Immunity Support

Any type of green vegetable contains natural compounds that help support a healthy immune system. The compounds enhance the proper function of immune cells in the gut and skin.

4. Portable Superfood

Greens are full of nutrients, especially when you eat them raw. Unlike most foods, greens have higher nutrient contents such as watercress. Including any of these greens in your diet can help you meet a few recommended daily nutrient intake.

5. Boost Fat Loss

Greens’ low-calorie content and detoxifying potential help with weight loss. Some also contain nutrients that promote fat burning and help curb your appetite.

List of Supergreens and Their Benefits

1. Kale

Kale is one of the most nutritious greens around. Its fiber content aids digestion and promotes cardiovascular health. It also improves blood glucose levels to help with diabetes, prevent asthma, lower blood pressure, and decrease the risk of developing cancer.

2. Spinach

Spinach is second to kale as one of the healthiest supergreens. It has a mild flavor but filled with tons of nutrients. 180 grams of spinach contains about 7 mg of iron.

3. Beet Greens

Beet greens are often taken for granted and thrown into the garbage disposal in favor of the beet root. The greens of this vegetable are actually a type of superfood filled with fiber.

4. Romaine Lettuce

The fluffy leaves of romaine lettuce contain high amounts of antioxidants which can help prevent cancer. Its vitamin B content helps fight depression and boost male fertility.

5. Leaf Lettuce

If you don’t have romaine lettuce, the classic leaf lettuce is just as nutritious. Include two cups of this leaf vegetable in your daily salad to meet your day’s recommended intake of vitamin K. Vitamin K is an essential nutrient for strong bones and helps promote bone damage.

6. Chicory

The ancient Egyptians used Chicory as traditional medicine. Today, it’s often used to enhance the flavor of coffee. This green is also an appetite stimulant and can help lower your cholesterol levels.

7. Chinese Cabbage

Chinese cabbage can help lower inflammation in women. It can deactivate inflammation markers in your body which also helps lower risks of heart disease.

8. Parsley

Parsley is full of antioxidants to help prevent diseases. Its vitamin K content also promotes bone health while its aroma helps you control your appetite.

9. Chard

Chard is a supergreen which can help fight type 2 diabetes. It’s full of different types of antioxidants that can help prevent inflammation, decrease insulin resistance, and aid in the regulation of blood glucose levels.

10. Watercress

Watercress has a nutrient score of 100 because every gram of this supergreen is laden with vitamins. It has more beta-carotene than apples and a serving can help meet your daily intake of vitamin K.

Dubbed as a “fountain of youth,” this supergreen promotes younger-looking and glowing skin. It also prevents the development of cancer and DNA damage.
