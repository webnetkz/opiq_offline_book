TIP #2: Make Your Workouts Seasonal

Embrace the change of seasons and take advantage of activities that you can only do in fall or winter!

TIP #3: Try Something Totally New

We aren’t saying you should become lazy! But if you have been crushing it 24/7 all spring and summer, your body (and mind) may need to slow down.

  • Commit to daily yoga.
  • Focus on becoming more flexible.
  • Switch to low or moderate-intensity cardio, instead of HIIT.
  • Try out some bodyweight workouts instead of heavy lifting.

The best way to stick to a plan is to have someone that you report back to. This could be a friend, family member, a social media community, or even a coach.

March may seem like a long time away, but it will be back before you know it, and 6 months is a long time to make significant changes.

The key to creating goals that stick .. be REALISTIC and SPECIFIC!

Let’s start with reality. You should always focus on one goal at a time and make sure you are being honest with yourself if that goal is really attainable.

If you know that your schedule will never allow you to work out 6 days a week, why would you choose a program that requires you to do that?

If you know it’s not healthy to attempt to lose 10 pounds a week, why would you aim that high?

If you love carbs, why would you go on a KETO diet? 

Doing these things will essentially set yourself up for failure. Set attainable goals that fit YOUR lifestyle. You can always bump things up if your schedule allows, or you notice you aren’t pushing yourself hard enough. Just keep in mind that being realistic isn’t the same as not believing in yourself or making excuses. 

Moving on to being specific. The more specific you are about your goals, the more likely you will be to reach them.

Post-Workout Nutrition | Its Benefits to Overall Fitness and Health

How is Post-Workout Nutrition Beneficial to One’s Overall Fitness and Health?

Post-workout nutrition is more important than many athletes realize. As important as pre-workout food is, it is also crucial to turn your attention to what you eat after working out. Post-workout meal planning is essential to help you get the most benefit from your workout.

Why is Eating the Right Post-Workout Food So Important?

During your workout, your body begins the process of proteolysis, or the breaking down muscle proteins. That process intensifies during the three hours after your workout. The post-workout food choices that are best for you are ones that accomplish the following two things:

  1. Minimize the breakdown of muscles post-workout.
  2. Stimulate protein synthesis to promote muscle growth.

Protein eaten at any time is essential for promoting muscle growth. Protein consumed post-workout packs a little more power in that muscle-boosting punch.

How much of a boost does it give?

According to a study, post-workout infusions of amino acids yielded 30 to 100 percent more protein synthesis than the same infusions administered while subjects were at rest.

That means that the right source of protein is essential post-workout food if you’re hoping to build muscle, even if lean muscle production is your goal.

What kinds of protein are best after working out?

The beautiful thing about protein is that there are so many options for getting quality protein – even on the go. These are some of the foods to consider for your after workout meal:

  • Greek yogurt
  • Eggs
  • Cottage cheese
  • Protein bars
  • Tuna
  • Salmon
  • Chicken

You can even use animal or plant-based protein powders to make smoothies for a quick boost of nutrient-rich, protein-packed energy after working out.

How much protein do you need to consume after a workout?

While the age old rule of thumb has been to consume a minimum of 20 grams of protein in after workout meals, modern wisdom shines new light on the subject.

This new information reveals that different people require different amounts of protein to optimize protein synthesis. Many experts now recommend between 40 and 50 grams of protein post-workout to achieve maximum protein synthesis.

Carbohydrates After Working Out?

With so much controversy over carbohydrates, it seems like this would be a taboo topic when it comes to effective post-workout foods. The truth is carbs help replenish depleted glycogen stores. Your body burns glycogen to fuel your workout. Carbs help to restore balance.

Not all workouts require as much help in restoring these depleted reserves. For instance, endurance sports and activities (running, swimming, cycling, etc.) need more compared to body building or resistance training.

Note: It is more important for people who engage in exercise more frequently (such as twice in the same day without days of rest between workouts) than for people who workout more sporadically.

For those who require the replenishment of glycogen stores, however, the general rule of thumb is to follow a carb to protein ratio of 3:1. This indicates three grams of carbs for every one gram of protein. Consuming the right carbs and proteins together stimulates insulin secretion which promotes optimal glycogen synthesis.

Also, consume this carb to protein mixture within 30 minutes of training to achieve the proper synthesis of glycogen.

Among the best post-workout carbs to consider are:

  • Sweet potatoes
  • Chocolate milk
  • Fruits
  • Rice cakes
  • Oatmeal
  • Potatoes
  • Pasta
  • Leafy green vegetables (the darker varieties)
  • Rice

These carbs are easily digestible and simple to prepare making them excellent choices for post-workout fuel.

Don’t Forget the Fats

Fat is often maligned today. However, fat is an essential component of a healthy diet. The thing to remember is that there are good fats and bad fats. Healthy fats to include in a well-rounded post-workout meal plan include:

  • Avocados
  • Nuts
  • Nut butters
  • Coconut oils
  • Fatty fish (like salmon)

The key is moderation and discretion when consuming fats after working out and at any other time. Focusing on healthy fats allows you to enjoy the benefits without many of the risks associated with unhealthy varieties of fats.

Timing Your Post-Workout Meals

Timing is everything when it comes to maximizing the impact of a good after workout meal. While some suggest eating within 30 minutes of working out, there are others who suggest you may have up to 45 minutes after you finish exercising to enjoy the benefits.

Ultimately, the sooner you begin restoring your body’s depleted stores, the better. That means that preparing meals ahead of time is your best bet or consider portable meals as part of your post-workout routine.

Keeping Track of This Year’s Thanksgiving Calories

Food

Keeping track of the calories you consume is important no matter what your fitness goal is. Here’s how much calories every Thanksgiving food item has.

Turkey

Serving size: 3 ounces (The size of a deck of cards)

Calories: 132 (white meat)

How many calories are in a slice of Thanksgiving turkey? Well, the calories in turkey dinner with all the trimmings are around 132 per 3 ounces. Take note that this is for white meat turkey. Dark meat turkey is much tastier but has more fats and calories.

Vegetables

Serving size: ½ cup (a rounded handful of chosen veggies)

Calories: Varies

A half cup of vegetables is the palate cleanser you need to wash off all the fats and grease you’re consuming. Stick to green, leafy vegetable for fewer calories and more vitamins and minerals.

Salad

Serving size: 1 cup (the size a baseball)

Calories: 100-150 (depending on the dressing)

A cup of salad usually has around 100 to 150 calories. Keep in mind that most pre-packaged dressings are extremely high in calories.

To cut back on Thanksgiving calories, opt to use low-calorie cheese or vinaigrette instead of traditional dressings such as thousand island.

Gravy

Serving size: ¼ cup (the size of a golf ball)

Calories: 30-50

Contrary to popular belief, gravy doesn’t contain that many calories. In fact, there are only around 30 to 50 calories in a ¼ cup of gravy.

Just make sure not to eat more than a cup to avoid consuming too much sodium.

Cranberry Sauce

Serving size: ¼ cup (the size of a golf ball)

Calories: 105

Cranberry sauce is another Thanksgiving favorite. However, when counting your Thanksgiving calories, keep in mind that cranberry sauce has more than double the amount of fat that gravy has.

Stuffing

Serving size: ½ cup (the size of a standard ice cream scoop)

Calories: 180

Try to keep your turkey stuffing down to less than half a cup. Going over that will just increase your Thanksgiving calories.

Potatoes (Sweet or Mashed)

Serving size: ½ cup (the size of half a tennis ball)

Calories: 145

Sweet and mashed potatoes are a must at any Thanksgiving dinner. Luckily, you won’t consume too many carbs and calories if you only eat half a cup.

Tip: Don’t drown the potatoes in gravy. Doing so not only lessens the flavor, but it also adds unnecessary Thanksgiving calories.

Cornbread (Without Butter)

Serving size: 1 medium muffin or square (the size of a bar of soap)

Calories: 175

A serving of cornbread without butter has around 175 calories. It’s best to limit yourself to one piece of cornbread.

Think about it, this pastry has more calories than a serving of white meat turkey.

Butter or Margarine

Serving size: 1 teaspoon (the size of a dice)

Calories: 36

Adding a teaspoon of butter or margarine on your cornbread will lead to an extra 36 calories.

Pie

Serving size: ⅛ of a 9” pie (the size of a standard light bulb)

Calories: 323 (pumpkin), 456 (pecan)

Thanksgiving day is the perfect opportunity for families to bust out their best pie recipe. However, if you want to cut back on the calories in your Thanksgiving dinner, stick to eating pumpkin pie.

But if you simply can’t resist the goodness of a pecan pie, just try to lessen the amount you’re eating.

Drinks

Let’s face it: no Thanksgiving dinner is complete without a few rounds of drinks. But keep in mind that alcoholic beverages add a ton to your Thanksgiving calories. Plus, they offer zero nutritional value.

However, Thanksgiving only comes once a year. So we can’t blame you for having a glass or two.

Overall, the best pick among these drinks is champagne. Meanwhile, try to steer clear of the sugary cocktail drinks and calorie-filled beer.

White Wine

Calories: 121 per glass

White wine is a great alcoholic drink that helps lower cholesterol and balance your blood sugar. After all the food you ate, a glass of white wine may be the cleanser you need.

Red Wine

Calories: 127 per glass

While it does have 127 calories per glass, red wine contains plenty of antioxidants that are helpful to the body. You can opt to have a glass or two this Thanksgiving.

Champagne

Calories: 78 per glass

Champagne drinks are very smooth and easy to drink. Plus, they only have 78 calories per glass. They’re the ideal alcoholic beverage to down this coming holiday season.

Regular Beer

Calories: 150 per bottle

Many of us are too lazy to do anything other than pop open a bottle of beer. But if you want to stick to your fitness goals, it’s best to lay off the regular beer this Thanksgiving.

Light Beer

Calories: 95 per bottle

If you really don’t want to drink anything other than beer, don’t worry! Opt for light beer instead. It packs as much punch and flavor as regular beer minus the extra calories and carbs.

Generic Cocktail

Calories: 155 per glass

This Thanksgiving, it’s best to skip out on these sugary cocktail drinks. They contain too many sweeteners and juice that add up to carb- and calorie-consumption.

11 Quick, Healthy Lunch Ideas to Keep You Fit

 

1. Tomato Panzanella

Calories: 485

Cooking Time: 45 minutes

If you’re looking for quick healthy lunch ideas to replace the boring pre-packaged salads from your local convenience store, then you’ve found it. This Tomato Panzanella recipe is a quick, healthy lunch salad you can whip up in just a matter of minutes.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 loaf sourdough bread
  • 2 large cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 6 anchovy fillets
  • 3 cups basil
  • 2 tbsp. sherry vinegar
  • 4 tbsp. unsalted butter
  • 2 lbs. heirloom tomatoes, sliced
  • Pepper
  • Kosher salt

How to:

  1. Tear the sourdough bread into large pieces and cut the crust off. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Throw in the garlic, ¼ cup of salt, and 1 ½ cups of basil in a food processor then pulse until it forms a rich, creamy blend. This’ll be the salad dressing.
  3. Toss the tomatoes in the dressing and let it stand in room temperature for around 30 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, throw a ¼ cup of oil into the saucepan and melt the butter in it. Then, coat the bread with the melted butter and arrange the slices in a large rimmed baking sheet. Bake for around 15 to 20 minutes.
  5. Place the tomatoes with the croutons, mix in the basil, and then seasons with oil and pepper.
  6. Serve and enjoy.

2. Herbed Ricotta and Fresh Tomato Tart

There are plenty of quick and easy lunch ideas to make you forget about your favorite pizza place. For example, this herbed ricotta and fresh tomato tart is a delicious, easy healthy lunch that gives you all the exciting flavors of a pizza, but minus the guilt.

Plus, it’s one of the best easy lunch recipes to eat after a workout. In fact, these healthy lunch foods pack about 10 grams of protein and only 25 grams of carbs per serving.

Calories: 300

Cooking Time: 40 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet frozen puff pastry, thawed
  • 2 scallions, chopped
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • Pepper
  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 lemon
  • 1/2 cup fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped
  • Flaky sea salt
  • 1 lb. heirloom tomatoes, sliced
  • 2 tbsp. olive oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves, small

How to:

  1. Preheat the oven to 425 degrees Fahrenheit and place the rack in the lower third of it Next, spread the pastry onto the parchment paper, leaving about half an inch of roll on all sides for the crust. Place the parchment on the baking sheet.
  2. Using a knife, slice a half-inch border all the way around the pastry.
  3. Brush the corners with the beaten egg and poke a hole in the pastry using a fork.
  4. Bake the pastry for 20 to 25 minutes
  5. Throw the ricotta, salt, pepper, and lemon juice in a large bowl then mix. Fold in the parsley and scallops then spread it on the pastry you just finished baking.
  6. Carefully place the tomatoes on the tarts then drizzle the oil, sea salt, and pepper.

3. Peanut Butter Banana Bread Granola

Calories: 350

Cooking Time: 50 minutes

Meal preppers short on time will love this peanut butter banana bread granola recipe. It’s one of the easiest healthy lunch recipes you can prepare ahead of time.

Prepare seven days’ worth of this healthy lunch over the weekend, then stock them in the refrigerator. That way, you can get your fix of protein and carbs any time.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup banana chips, crushed
  • 1/2 cup ripe banana, mashed
  • 3 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 cup salted peanuts
  • 1/2 cup uncooked quinoa
  • 1/4 cup packed light brown sugar

How To Meal Prep For A Healthier Life

The Benefits of Meal Prepping

There are many benefits to learning how to meal prep both for your wallet and for your health. Some of the best benefits of meal prepping include:

  • Staying on your dietary routine – Whether you follow nutrition goals for muscle growth, weight loss, or portion control-related to health concerns, meal preparing can make it easier to stay on track and reduce the temptation.
  • Reducing the amount of preparation – While the initial prep of your weekly meals may seem like a significant amount of time, you are saving hours by not having to plan and prep your meals each night. You will also likely reduce emergency trips to the store for missing ingredients.
  • Staying on a budget – When you plan your meals in advance you will be less tempted to buy things you don’t need or will not use throughout the week. Additionally, you will be able to take advantage of bulk purchasing and buying larger quantities of items when they are on sale.

What Foods Are Best for Meal Prep?

Meal preparation is almost no different than storing leftovers. So a good rule of thumb is, if you won’t keep it as a leftover, it might not be the best for meal prep.

That being said there are definitely foods that are better choices for meal preparation. Some of the best choices include:

1. Grains

2. Pasta

3. Beans

4. Meat

5. Roasted vegetables

6. Hearty raw vegetables such as carrots, peas, and peppers

7. Whole fruits such as apples and oranges

8. Nuts

9. Cheese

10. Sauces such as salad dressings and sour cream

11. Dips such as hummus and salsa

Foods that do not last so well for meal preparation include soft or cut fruits and vegetables and foods that are supposed to be crunchy such as crackers and chips. There are some option to include these items such as individual packaging.

What Do You Need to Start?

Before learning how to meal prep, you will need to gather the items necessary to make your prep as easy and efficient as possible.

To make prepping most effective, the primary items you will need are containers. The best types of containers are ones that are dishwasher and microwave safe and are able to go into the freezer. You also want to make sure that you get the containers in a variety of sizes.

Consider investing in some high quality separated containers to keep a whole meal in one place. Buy multiple sizes of resealable bags for ingredients that need to be separated out.

Once you have gathered your needed containers, you will want to get together items you will need to cook your meals. Gather baking dishes, pots and pans, and even crock pots and steamers to be able to get as many items preparing as you can.

 

Getting Started

There are a few steps you need to take to begin prepping like a pro. It is important to remember that proper planning is the key to successful meal preparation.

Pick Your Prep Method

Determining your prep method will largely depend on what the goals of your meal preparation are. There are four primary prep methods to choose from including:

1. Make-ahead meals – These meals are completely cooked before they are stored and then reheated when it is time to eat. This is a good option for those who want to keep to a diet or have home-cooked meals with little preparation.

2. Batch cooking and freeze storing – In this method, batches of food are cooked. Then they are portioned out and frozen to be used for meals later. This is a good option for recipes that are easy to make larger and ones that reheat well.

3. Pre-portioned meals – This is the best option for those with a strict diet or specified nutrient needs. When you prepare this way, you will be creating grab and go meals that take the guesswork out of portioning and calorie counting.

Create Your Meal Plan

After determining your prep type, you will need to create your menu and create a meal plan.

1. Decide which meals you want to prep.

2. Determine your menu, making sure to plan your meals around seasonal options.

3. Schedule your designated prep time making sure you will have plenty of time to get everything done uninterrupted.

Get Shopping

Once your menu is planned, it is time to get the ingredients you will need for your meal prep. Make sure that your kitchen is stocked with most of the staples that the recipes call for including spices and other seasonings.

Next, you will want to create a detailed grocery list, organizing it by grocery department. This will not only reduce the amount of time you have to spend in the store but also help you remember everything you need.

Begin Prepping and Storing

Now that you have everything ready to go, it is time to get cooking. Cooking your meals will largely depend on what recipes you use, but always look for ways to be effective such as baking two dishes that cook at the same temperature together.

Meal Prepping Tips

To get the most out of your meal prep follow the few easy tips below.

1. Use your time wisely – Try to plan your time as efficiently as possible making sure to start cooking items that will take the longest. For recipes that call for the same ingredients, prep those meals at the same time, dividing the ingredients as you need. You can also save time cleaning your cutting board by cutting all of your raw meats first.

2. Keep storage life in mind – You don’t want all of your hard work to be for nothing. Make sure that you know the prep methods to keep items as long as possible and only prep out as long as the ingredients will stay fresh.

3. Freeze properly – Freezing can be a great way to get ahead on meals and make sure they stay fresh longer. Making sure your foods are properly prepped and packaged to freeze properly is important. For example, you should always leave an inch on top of containers or bags for foods to expand while they freeze. Also, make sure to properly label and date everything, so you never wonder how old or what something is.

The Three Body Types

For sustainable long-term weight loss, the best approach is to figure out your body type and choose your diet accordingly. There are three main body types.

  • Ectomorphs — Featuring small bone structures, ectomorphs tend to be “naturally” thin with fast metabolisms. They also tend to have small chests and buttocks, with a straight, almost ruler-like shape. They may be hyperactive and sometimes have difficulty gaining weight or muscle mass. Think Calista Flockhart, Kate Moss, Audrey Hepburn, or Matthew McConaughey.
  • Mesomorphs — Boasting a medium bone structure, mesomorphs tend to be athletic and lean. They also tend to have higher levels of testosterone and growth hormones, which allow them to develop muscle easily.
  • Male mesomorphs tend to be wider at the shoulders than the hips, and women often have an hourglass shape. Famous people with mesomorphic qualities include Angela Bassett, Madonna, George Clooney, and Bruce Willis.
  • Endomorphs — The total opposite of ectomorphs, endomorphs have a larger frame with a soft and curvy body. They tend to be pear-shaped, with wider hips than shoulders. Since they have sluggish metabolisms, they need to work hard to lose extra fat they gain. On the other hand, they boast some serious muscle when they get control of their physique. Some examples of celebrities with endomorph bodies are Roseanne Barr and Jonah Hill, but also curvy frames like Marilyn Monroe and J Lo, and last but not least – athletes such as most NFL linemen.

Keep in mind these body types may have some overlap, and they can be confused with each other. For instance, an endomorph who has spent a lot of time weightlifting and working out may look like a naturally muscular mesomorph.

Similarly, if an ectomorph has a very sedentary lifestyle, they can put on weight and end up looking like an endomorph. Often, you can tell the difference because ectomorphs tend to have relatively thin limbs, even if they have put weight on around the middle of their bodies.

Ectomorphs Best Diets

Due to their high metabolism, ectomorphs tend to have a high tolerance for carbohydrates. In fact, you need a lot of carbs to make up the calories you’re likely burning with your high activity levels.

The best diet for this body type is a low-fat diet featuring moderate levels of protein.

As a guideline, your calories should come 55% from carbs, 25% from protein, and 20% from fat. A sample meal that meets those proportions includes a portion of meat about the size of your palm, a fist-sized vegetable serving, two handfuls of dense carbs, and fat that’s about half your thumb.

For instance, a chicken breast, a small scoop of vegetables, a cup of rice, and a pat of butter meets these criteria. Note the exact portions vary based on your body weight and your weight loss goals.

Mesomorph Best Diet

Mesomorphs thrive with a balanced diet. Shoot for 45% carbs, 25% protein, and 30% fat.

On the plate, this ideal meal looks like a palm-sized serving of protein, a fist-sized scoop of vegetables, a cupped handful of carbs, and about a thumb worth of dense fats. That’s essentially the equivalent of a four-ounce piece of lean steak, a serving of your favorite vegetables, a half cup of quinoa, and a pat of butter.

Again, remember that the exact portions vary based on your body size.

If you’re trying to build muscle, you may need to increase your protein intake as well, especially if you have an ectomorphic body type.
  • Moderate Carb Tolerance — This tolerance level is often associated with the mesomorph body type. You should only eat a lot of carbs around workouts, but otherwise, only eat a moderate amount of carbs and focus on lean protein, fruits, and vegetables.
  • Low Carb Tolerance — If your carb tolerance is low, you should only have carbs if you’re working out and ideally near your workout times. If you’re trying to lose weight, you may want to cut out most carbs and embrace a low-carb diet with lots of fruits, vegetables, protein, and some fat.

The healthiest carbohydrates feature complex whole grains, and they include rice, quinoa, millet, corn, and barley. Many vegetables and even fruits are also carb-rich, such as yams, sweet potatoes, and bananas.

When creating a meal plan for yourself, try to focus on healthy carbs rather than refined sugar or white flour.

Also, remember that the sugars in fruit aren’t necessarily healthy and try to strike the right ratio between your fruits and vegetables. As a general rule of thumb, you may want to focus on the following fruit and vegetable ratios based on your body type and car tolerance:

  • Mesomorphs / Moderate Carb Tolerance: Four vegetable servings to one fruit serving
  • Endomorph / Low Carb Tolerance: Five vegetables to one fruit serving

When you’re looking at diet plans for weight loss, you also need to think about your personal preferences and your lifestyle. To explain, imagine that a high protein diet works best for your body type, but you’re a vegan.
