TIP #2: Make Your Workouts Seasonal

Embrace the change of seasons and take advantage of activities that you can only do in fall or winter! 

  • Go on a fall hike and view all the gorgeous foliage.
  • Try rock climbing.
  • Walk around a corn maze or pumpkin patch with your family. 
  • If you live in a snowy climate, go skiing, sledding, build snowmen with your kids, etc.
  • Go ice skating.
  • Lace-up your sneakers, go for a walk or run at night, and admire all the Christmas lights and decorations.

There are some things you can only do and see during specific times of the year. So instead of missing sunshine and water sports, focus on all the positive things that only come about with cooler temps!

TIP #3: Try Something Totally New

Sometimes a change of routine is all you need to boost motivation.

If you have been doing all your gym lifts alone, recruit a gym buddy! Try switching up your cardio by skipping your solo runs and joining a group fitness class instead. 

Another option is to allow yourself to slow down! If you have been crushing it all spring and summer with intense training and hardcore workouts, it may actually be time to take it down a notch. 

Going full force 365 can cause your stress hormone cortisol to increase. This can lead to a host of drawbacks, including weight gain, muscle loss, poor moods, fatigue, and occasional issues sleeping! 

We aren’t saying you should become lazy! But if you have been crushing it 24/7 all spring and summer, your body (and mind) may need to slow down.

Use the next few months to embrace a slower routine. 

  • Commit to daily yoga.
  • Focus on becoming more flexible.
  • Switch to low or moderate-intensity cardio, instead of HIIT.
  • Try out some bodyweight workouts instead of heavy lifting.

Giving your body a couple of months to recover might even make you more responsive when you decide to kick things up again come spring/summer!

TIP #4: Stay Accountable


The best way to stick to a plan is to have someone that you report back to. This could be a friend, family member, a social media community, or even a coach. 

Including someone else, or even just telling someone else about your health and fitness goals and asking them to check in with you to make sure you are sticking to the plan, is a great way to keep the promises you make to yourself.

If you prefer to fly solo, it helps to create visuals. Make a vision board, create a daily checklist you can mark off after each workout, or keep an accomplishment journal.

TIP #5: Set A Spring Goal

March may seem like a long time away, but it will be back before you know it, and 6 months is a long time to make significant changes. 

Setting a goal is a great way to encourage yourself to push past discomforts and temptation, but there is a science to goal setting.

The key to creating goals that stick .. be REALISTIC and SPECIFIC!

Let’s start with reality. You should always focus on one goal at a time and make sure you are being honest with yourself if that goal is really attainable.

If you know that your schedule will never allow you to work out 6 days a week, why would you choose a program that requires you to do that?

If you know it’s not healthy to attempt to lose 10 pounds a week, why would you aim that high?

If you love carbs, why would you go on a KETO diet? 

Doing these things will essentially set yourself up for failure. Set attainable goals that fit YOUR lifestyle. You can always bump things up if your schedule allows, or you notice you aren’t pushing yourself hard enough. Just keep in mind that being realistic isn’t the same as not believing in yourself or making excuses. 

Moving on to being specific. The more specific you are about your goals, the more likely you will be to reach them. 

It may seem like it’s good enough to say things like .. I want to look good, lose weight, or get ripped! But you have to dig deeper than that!! 

Wanting to “lose weight,” for example, is an excellent place to start. But ask yourself what that means to you?

If you expand on that and say — “I want to lose 10 pounds of fat. I will do this by not eating any sweets and doing the V Shred MOVE program for 30 consecutive days. I am doing this because I want to have better self-esteem and a head start on bikini season.” — that makes your goal more specific.  

The more specific (and attainable) your goals are, the more likely you will be to not give up!

TIP #6: Reward Yourself

Once you have checked a “mini goal” off your list, treat yourself. Knowing that you have a reward coming to celebrate your efforts will make you want to achieve it even more. 

It may be tempting to make your reward one of your favorite “forbidden foods” but we never suggest that you reward yourself this way.

You aren’t a dog. You should never reward “good behavior” with food. Having this mentality can create a really unhealthy relationship with food.

You need to eat healthy MOST of the time, control your portions, and see food as fuel. But if you are craving something that isn’t quite as healthy as your grilled chicken and rice, never feel like you need to lose 20 pounds to “deserve” a bite of cake, or nice dinner out with your family. 

Instead of rewarding yourself with food, buy yourself something you have been wanting. Maybe some new workout clothes, a massage, or an extra $20 bucks in your vacation fund!

The dark and dreary days of winter can also cause your body to produce more of the sleep hormone melatonin. This shift can disrupt your body and impact your sleep patterns and overall mood.

Here are a few ways that you can focus on improving your mental health.

  • Invest in a light therapy box that simulates natural daylight.
  • Make sure you are supplementing with or eating plenty of foods with Vitamin D.
  • Start journaling. There is a lot of scientific evidence that supports putting your thoughts on paper provides a long list of benefits, including stress management, self-confidence, and goal setting.

]]> Choosing the right pre-workout supplement can help increase the benefits of your exercise and aid you in building lean muscle. While there are many “wonder pills” on the market that guarantee you bulk with the pop of a pill, getting a healthy and toned body requires more effort.

You have to put in the exercise to see the results you want. You also have to provide your body with the right supplements and nutrients to build the muscle and burn the fat.

When choosing the right pre-workout supplements it is vital to get what your body needs and avoid the things it doesn’t.

What You Should Be Looking For In Your Workout Supplement

In This Article:

  • What Your Workout Supplement Should Have
  • What Your Workout Supplement Should Not Have

What Your Workout Supplement Should Have

There are many things your body will need to best enhance your workout process. Some of the things that you should find in the supplement you choose include:

1. Caffeine

Caffeine can provide you with the energy your body needs to work out. It also increases blood flow which allows your muscles to get the blood and nutrients they need.

Additionally, caffeine increases anaerobic capacity and muscle endurance. This will provide you with a more effective workout.

2. Branched Chain Amino Acids

Amino acids produce nitric oxide. This is an important supplement for increasing blood flow and providing the body with the extra oxygen it needs.

These ingredients are also shown to improve muscle strength and growth. Amino acids help you workout harder, longer, and faster.

They also help your body repair itself quicker creating less downtime after your workout.

3. Creatine

Creatine is a naturally occurring acid that is already present in the body. When used in a synthetic form, creatine can boost the production of ATP.

ATP will aid your muscle in repairing itself and growing when it is under stress. Creatine is one of the best performance enhancing supplements.

Creatine will give users an extra burst of energy that can help them to push a little further during their workout routine.

4. Citrulline

Citrulline is an amino acid organic compound that can help boost performance when working out.

Some studies show that using citrulline malate can assist in maintaining high performance over longer periods of time.

5. Glutamine

Glutamine is an amino acid that your body will produce. It comes out of your muscles during stressful situations such as during workouts.

By adding this supplement before your workout, you will recover the amount your body depletes and help maintain your muscle mass. Often times you will need to take glutamine in supplement form because it is naturally found in meat, beans, fish, and nuts.

These are not food best eaten right before working out.

6. Whey Protein Powder

Whey protein is often associated with fueling workout regiments. Though with its popularity comes a lot of confusion about the benefits it provides.

The truth is that protein powders will serve a variety of different purposes. There are some designed for those who are trying to stay lean and build lean muscles.

Others have large amounts of protein as well as calories to help build muscle mass. No matter which protein you choose make sure that it contains whey protein.

Whey protein contains ingredients proven to promote muscle protein synthesis. Choose ones that have lower calories and fewer ingredients to make sure you are getting the nutrition you need.

You should also make sure to adjust the amount you consume based on your body weight. Often times you may need two or three shakes a day combined with your workout.

Always remember though that these are supplements and you should get your primary protein from food.

7. Beta-Alanine

Beta-Alanine is an amino acid that reduces muscle fatigue. It does this by boosting your body’s natural carnosine.

This substance helps prevent the buildup of acid which can cause muscle fatigue, strain, and corrosion. Your body only has and can only produce so much carnosine based on the beta-alanine in your system.

Taking the supplement in addition to what your body produces will help you produce more carnosine. Beta-Alanine is often felt after a workout with a slight tingling sensation in your skin.

This tingling means that your body is reducing muscle fatigue by increasing your endurance making your cardiovascular fitness more productive.

8. Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates is often considered a dirty word when it comes to losing weight and dieting. While it is true that you should cut carbs to lose fat, if your goal is to burn fat and increase muscle, carbs will give you the fuel you need.

You should make sure to consume carbs once when you wake up and once before you workout each day. This will allow you to turn the carbs into energy and reduce the risk of it turning into fat consumed by the body.

After your work out the carbs will release sugar increasing your glucose level. Higher glucose levels will reduce the amount of cortisol excreted that can consume lean muscle tissue.

While you can gain your carbohydrates from consuming a variety of simple carbs sometimes it is easier to regulate your carb intake through the use of supplements.

What Your Workout Supplement Should Not Have

While there are many things you want to make sure are in your supplements there are also things that you want to avoid. Avoiding certain things can help you get the right nutrients for your goals and body size. Things you should try to avoid include:

1. Ingredients That are Under-Dosed

There is much research about how ingredients can be effective and what you need to consume to gain the benefits. Check the label and make sure that the items included are within the recommended dosage to be effective.

There is no reason to pay high prices for an ingredient with not enough milligrams for you to see the benefits.

2. Dyes and Artificial Sweeteners

There have been many studies on the ill effects of food dyes and artificial sweeteners. These items have no reason for consumption and provide no benefit.

Check for healthier alternatives when you see sweeteners or dyes on the label.

3. Unnecessary Ingredients

Sometimes a workout supplement can contain additional ingredients that are actually useless when consumed. Companies often feel that by offering more ingredients, the consumer is getting more for their money. In truth, you will end up consuming a lot of things that you don’t need.

4. Using Chemical Jargon

Some companies will try to sound more technical by using the chemical names of certain ingredients. They do this because consumers sometimes equate scientific words with better ingredients. In truth, most of these are common ingredients. Knowing what they are is important in knowing the benefits to expect as well as what you are consuming. Technical jargon can create a false impression of the product and is often used as a deceptive technique.

Vitamins for Weight Loss | How They Help Shed the Excess Pounds

 

1. Reduction of Chronic Inflammation


One of the benefits of vitamins for weight loss is the reduction of chronic inflammation. Obesity and inflammation have a complex and delicate relationship. Suffice to say, when the body is in this state, it also boosts the production of free radicals.

These free radicals can cause cellular damage. It then leads to a series of conditions that can impact your weight loss. They also lower your immune function. Getting sick all the time can hamper your workout plans.

Many factors can result in chronic inflammation. One of the reasons is nutrient deficiency. While vitamins themselves cannot lead to fat weight loss, they can improve it.

2. Response to Insulin

Insulin is a naturally occurring hormone. It’s one of the most important ones too.

When you eat food, the body converts some parts of it into blood sugar or glucose. The body then needs to deliver it to the cells for their different functions. They do not receive this sugar immediately, though.

This is where insulin comes in. Produced by the pancreas, it introduces glucose to the cells. Picture it as a messenger knocking on the doors of the cells.

There are certain situations when the cells do not receive glucose despite the presence of insulin. It then forces the organ to produce more of the hormone while the supply of blood sugar increases. In turn, it results in diabetes.

The vitamins for weight loss are not enough to treat the condition, but some of them can improve insulin sensitivity. These include vitamin D.

The same vitamin also enhances the function of leptin. It’s a hormone that helps tell your brain you’re already full (or starving). You can then pair this vitamin with apple cider vinegar to further enhance the effect.

3. Energy


The best way to lose weight always involves a good nutrition and exercise. The problem with the latter is you can lose some of the essential vitamins in your body.

When you’re on an intense workout plan, it’s best to invest in vitamins for weight loss and energy. These include the B vitamins.

B vitamins are of different types. Some of these are riboflavin (B6), choline, and cobalamin (B12). They perform different jobs in the body. They, though, tend to work toward your level of energy and metabolism.

Vitamin B12 for weight loss help break down fats, carbohydrates, and proteins. They improve the delivery of oxygen to the brain and cells to let them function at an optimum level. These can also help in enhancing the function of the muscles.

4. Thyroid Health

There are several reasons why you’re not losing weight. One of these is thyroid dysfunction, especially hypothyroidism. It refers to the sluggish or low production of thyroid hormones.

Thyroid problems can have many different causes. The solutions too may include multiple approaches.

You can take vitamins for weight loss and metabolism as part of the plan. One of the vitamins that promote weight loss and healthy thyroid is vitamin B2 or riboflavin. It can prevent the suppression of the hormone production.

5. Reduction of Fat Absorption


Do you know the body has different types of fat? The one in the middle, for example, is visceral fat. Contrary to popular belief, your body needs fat. It doesn’t only keep you warm. It stores your reserves.

One of the best natural supplements for weight loss can be calcium. Granted, it’s a mineral more than a vitamin, but it works symbiotically with vitamin D. The latter increases the body’s absorption of the mineral. Many also tend to consider calcium as a vitamin now.

How can calcium aid in weight loss? A Tennessee study suggested it affects the way the cells burn fat. The more calcium there is in the cell, the more fat it tends to burn.

It also reduces the amount of fat your body absorbs. It binds to the fat in your intestinal tract and prevents it from reaching your bloodstream.

6. Improved Fat-Burning Abilities

One of the vitamins to lose belly fat fast can be vitamin C. For the longest time, it’s associated with an improved immune function. More studies show it can do more.

For one, it encourages your body to produce collagen. Collagen is one of the most abundant types of protein in the muscles and tendons. It helps create the structure. As a person gets older, the production of collagen also decreases. They also lose their muscle mass.

By increasing the production of collagen, the body can less likely lose muscle mass. Muscles, on the other hand, tend to burn more calories than fat. This protein also significantly improves the feeling of satiety.

Vitamin C can also help your body burn fat during an exercise routine. Its presence in the bloodstream means your body burns 30 percent more fat during exercise.

Best Vitamins for Weight Loss: 5 Vitamins and Minerals that Will Help You Shed Some Pounds

The best weight loss results from calcium can be achieved through regular servings of low-fat dairy products, green leafy vegetables, and soy.

Magnesium functions mainly for energy production, conduction of nerve impulse, and muscle contraction. But, its role for the body is not limited to those.

Magnesium has been found out to be a good regulator of insulin and blood sugar levels. Thus, it plays an active role in controlling the risk of weight gain or obesity.
