TIP #2: Make Your Workouts Seasonal

Embrace the change of seasons and take advantage of activities that you can only do in fall or winter! 

  • Go on a fall hike and view all the gorgeous foliage.
  • Try rock climbing.
  • Walk around a corn maze or pumpkin patch with your family. 
  • If you live in a snowy climate, go skiing, sledding, build snowmen with your kids, etc.
  • Go ice skating.
  • Lace-up your sneakers, go for a walk or run at night, and admire all the Christmas lights and decorations.

There are some things you can only do and see during specific times of the year. So instead of missing sunshine and water sports, focus on all the positive things that only come about with cooler temps!

TIP #3: Try Something Totally New

Sometimes a change of routine is all you need to boost motivation.

If you have been doing all your gym lifts alone, recruit a gym buddy! Try switching up your cardio by skipping your solo runs and joining a group fitness class instead. 

Another option is to allow yourself to slow down! If you have been crushing it all spring and summer with intense training and hardcore workouts, it may actually be time to take it down a notch. 

Going full force 365 can cause your stress hormone cortisol to increase. This can lead to a host of drawbacks, including weight gain, muscle loss, poor moods, fatigue, and occasional issues sleeping! 

We aren’t saying you should become lazy! But if you have been crushing it 24/7 all spring and summer, your body (and mind) may need to slow down.

Use the next few months to embrace a slower routine. 

  • Commit to daily yoga.
  • Focus on becoming more flexible.
  • Switch to low or moderate-intensity cardio, instead of HIIT.
  • Try out some bodyweight workouts instead of heavy lifting.

Giving your body a couple of months to recover might even make you more responsive when you decide to kick things up again come spring/summer!

TIP #4: Stay Accountable


The best way to stick to a plan is to have someone that you report back to. This could be a friend, family member, a social media community, or even a coach. 

Including someone else, or even just telling someone else about your health and fitness goals and asking them to check in with you to make sure you are sticking to the plan, is a great way to keep the promises you make to yourself.

If you prefer to fly solo, it helps to create visuals. Make a vision board, create a daily checklist you can mark off after each workout, or keep an accomplishment journal.

TIP #5: Set A Spring Goal

March may seem like a long time away, but it will be back before you know it, and 6 months is a long time to make significant changes. 

Setting a goal is a great way to encourage yourself to push past discomforts and temptation, but there is a science to goal setting.

The key to creating goals that stick .. be REALISTIC and SPECIFIC!

Let’s start with reality. You should always focus on one goal at a time and make sure you are being honest with yourself if that goal is really attainable.

If you know that your schedule will never allow you to work out 6 days a week, why would you choose a program that requires you to do that?

If you know it’s not healthy to attempt to lose 10 pounds a week, why would you aim that high?

If you love carbs, why would you go on a KETO diet? 

Doing these things will essentially set yourself up for failure. Set attainable goals that fit YOUR lifestyle. You can always bump things up if your schedule allows, or you notice you aren’t pushing yourself hard enough. Just keep in mind that being realistic isn’t the same as not believing in yourself or making excuses. 

Moving on to being specific. The more specific you are about your goals, the more likely you will be to reach them. 

It may seem like it’s good enough to say things like .. I want to look good, lose weight, or get ripped! But you have to dig deeper than that!! 

Wanting to “lose weight,” for example, is an excellent place to start. But ask yourself what that means to you?

If you expand on that and say — “I want to lose 10 pounds of fat. I will do this by not eating any sweets and doing the V Shred MOVE program for 30 consecutive days. I am doing this because I want to have better self-esteem and a head start on bikini season.” — that makes your goal more specific.  

The more specific (and attainable) your goals are, the more likely you will be to not give up!

TIP #6: Reward Yourself

Once you have checked a “mini goal” off your list, treat yourself. Knowing that you have a reward coming to celebrate your efforts will make you want to achieve it even more. 

It may be tempting to make your reward one of your favorite “forbidden foods” but we never suggest that you reward yourself this way.

You aren’t a dog. You should never reward “good behavior” with food. Having this mentality can create a really unhealthy relationship with food.

You need to eat healthy MOST of the time, control your portions, and see food as fuel. But if you are craving something that isn’t quite as healthy as your grilled chicken and rice, never feel like you need to lose 20 pounds to “deserve” a bite of cake, or nice dinner out with your family. 

Instead of rewarding yourself with food, buy yourself something you have been wanting. Maybe some new workout clothes, a massage, or an extra $20 bucks in your vacation fund!

Earlier, we mentioned that cold weather motivation dips may not be your fault.

I’m sure you have heard of winter blues. Well, it’s a real thing. Less natural light can cause dips in serotonin, the neurotransmitter that regulates mood.

The dark and dreary days of winter can also cause your body to produce more of the sleep hormone melatonin. This shift can disrupt your body and impact your sleep patterns and overall mood.

  • Make sure you are supplementing with or eating plenty of foods with Vitamin D.

Here are 5 Tips To Help You Grill Out GUILT FREE …

HEALTHY BBQ TIP #1: FIRE UP THE GRILL

If your BBQ includes grilling .. you are in luck. This is one of the best ways to cook meat or make some veggies. You use much less (if any) oil on a grill, as opposed to sautéing or roasting. Grilling alone will really help lower your 4th of July calorie intake.

HEALTHY BBQ TIP #2: PICK LEAN PROTEINS

Hitting your protein goals on the 4th shouldn’t be a problem. You just need to be careful what cut of meat you choose.

A small burger patty or steak could easily set you back 25 grams of fat or more. Most hot dogs have a minimum of 14 grams of fat. Of course, that is before all the condiments and cheese.

You can still have a juicy, delicious burger, or hot dog, you just need to pick a leaner cut.

Examples of lean cuts of beef are … 96/4 or 90/10 ground sirloin, eye of round steak, sirloin tip steak, top round steak, bottom round steak, and top sirloin steak

For hot dogs .. there are a lot of low-fat or even fat-free franks on the market. You can also opt for a turkey or chicken hot dog. Just make sure you look at the nutrition label. People often think that turkey and chicken are automatically the most calorie-friendly choice. Sometimes they can be just as calorie filled as the original.

You can always skip the beef and go with other lean meats like chicken breast, white fish, shrimp, or even salmon.

HEALTHY BBQ TIP #3: DITCH THE BUN & CHOOSE CONDIMENTS WISELY

What’s a burger or hot dog without a bun? Actually .. they can be just as delicious without all those extra carbs. A bun usually contains about 200 calories.

Why not save those calories for some delicious sides like potato salad? Or an adult beverage, like a watermelon tonic?

BBQ sauce and mayonnaise can be calorie bombs. A couple tiny spoonfuls could set you back hundreds of calories. Believe it or not, even ketchup is pretty calorie-dense.

Mustard, salsa, horseradish, and hot sauce are great choices. There are some great low-sugar or lower calorie bbq and ketchup alternatives out there. Look for those when you do your 4th of July grocery haul!

Post-Workout Nutrition | Its Benefits to Overall Fitness and Health

How is Post-Workout Nutrition Beneficial to One’s Overall Fitness and Health?

Post-workout nutrition is more important than many athletes realize. As important as pre-workout food is, it is also crucial to turn your attention to what you eat after working out. Post-workout meal planning is essential to help you get the most benefit from your workout.

Why is Eating the Right Post-Workout Food So Important?

During your workout, your body begins the process of proteolysis, or the breaking down muscle proteins. That process intensifies during the three hours after your workout. The post-workout food choices that are best for you are ones that accomplish the following two things:

  1. Minimize the breakdown of muscles post-workout.
  2. Stimulate protein synthesis to promote muscle growth.

Protein eaten at any time is essential for promoting muscle growth. Protein consumed post-workout packs a little more power in that muscle-boosting punch.

How much of a boost does it give?

According to a study, post-workout infusions of amino acids yielded 30 to 100 percent more protein synthesis than the same infusions administered while subjects were at rest.

That means that the right source of protein is essential post-workout food if you’re hoping to build muscle, even if lean muscle production is your goal.

What kinds of protein are best after working out?

The beautiful thing about protein is that there are so many options for getting quality protein – even on the go. These are some of the foods to consider for your after workout meal:

  • Greek yogurt
  • Eggs
  • Cottage cheese
  • Protein bars
  • Tuna
  • Salmon
  • Chicken

You can even use animal or plant-based protein powders to make smoothies for a quick boost of nutrient-rich, protein-packed energy after working out.

How much protein do you need to consume after a workout?

While the age old rule of thumb has been to consume a minimum of 20 grams of protein in after workout meals, modern wisdom shines new light on the subject.

This new information reveals that different people require different amounts of protein to optimize protein synthesis. Many experts now recommend between 40 and 50 grams of protein post-workout to achieve maximum protein synthesis.

Carbohydrates After Working Out?

With so much controversy over carbohydrates, it seems like this would be a taboo topic when it comes to effective post-workout foods. The truth is carbs help replenish depleted glycogen stores. Your body burns glycogen to fuel your workout. Carbs help to restore balance.

Not all workouts require as much help in restoring these depleted reserves. For instance, endurance sports and activities (running, swimming, cycling, etc.) need more compared to body building or resistance training.

Note: It is more important for people who engage in exercise more frequently (such as twice in the same day without days of rest between workouts) than for people who workout more sporadically.

For those who require the replenishment of glycogen stores, however, the general rule of thumb is to follow a carb to protein ratio of 3:1. This indicates three grams of carbs for every one gram of protein. Consuming the right carbs and proteins together stimulates insulin secretion which promotes optimal glycogen synthesis.

Also, consume this carb to protein mixture within 30 minutes of training to achieve the proper synthesis of glycogen.

Among the best post-workout carbs to consider are:

  • Sweet potatoes
  • Chocolate milk
  • Fruits
  • Rice cakes
  • Oatmeal
  • Potatoes
  • Pasta
  • Leafy green vegetables (the darker varieties)
  • Rice

These carbs are easily digestible and simple to prepare making them excellent choices for post-workout fuel.

Don’t Forget the Fats

Fat is often maligned today. However, fat is an essential component of a healthy diet. The thing to remember is that there are good fats and bad fats. Healthy fats to include in a well-rounded post-workout meal plan include:

  • Avocados
  • Nuts
  • Nut butters
  • Coconut oils
  • Fatty fish (like salmon)

The key is moderation and discretion when consuming fats after working out and at any other time. Focusing on healthy fats allows you to enjoy the benefits without many of the risks associated with unhealthy varieties of fats.

Timing Your Post-Workout Meals

Timing is everything when it comes to maximizing the impact of a good after workout meal. While some suggest eating within 30 minutes of working out, there are others who suggest you may have up to 45 minutes after you finish exercising to enjoy the benefits.

Ultimately, the sooner you begin restoring your body’s depleted stores, the better. That means that preparing meals ahead of time is your best bet or consider portable meals as part of your post-workout routine.

Keeping Track of This Year’s Thanksgiving Calories

Food

Keeping track of the calories you consume is important no matter what your fitness goal is. Here’s how much calories every Thanksgiving food item has.

Turkey

Serving size: 3 ounces (The size of a deck of cards)

Calories: 132 (white meat)

How many calories are in a slice of Thanksgiving turkey? Well, the calories in turkey dinner with all the trimmings are around 132 per 3 ounces. Take note that this is for white meat turkey. Dark meat turkey is much tastier but has more fats and calories.

Vegetables

Serving size: ½ cup (a rounded handful of chosen veggies)

Calories: Varies

A half cup of vegetables is the palate cleanser you need to wash off all the fats and grease you’re consuming. Stick to green, leafy vegetable for fewer calories and more vitamins and minerals.

Salad

Serving size: 1 cup (the size a baseball)

Calories: 100-150 (depending on the dressing)

A cup of salad usually has around 100 to 150 calories. Keep in mind that most pre-packaged dressings are extremely high in calories.

To cut back on Thanksgiving calories, opt to use low-calorie cheese or vinaigrette instead of traditional dressings such as thousand island.

Gravy

Serving size: ¼ cup (the size of a golf ball)

Calories: 30-50

Contrary to popular belief, gravy doesn’t contain that many calories. In fact, there are only around 30 to 50 calories in a ¼ cup of gravy.

Just make sure not to eat more than a cup to avoid consuming too much sodium.

Cranberry Sauce

Serving size: ¼ cup (the size of a golf ball)

Calories: 105

Cranberry sauce is another Thanksgiving favorite. However, when counting your Thanksgiving calories, keep in mind that cranberry sauce has more than double the amount of fat that gravy has.

Stuffing

Serving size: ½ cup (the size of a standard ice cream scoop)

Calories: 180

Try to keep your turkey stuffing down to less than half a cup. Going over that will just increase your Thanksgiving calories.

Potatoes (Sweet or Mashed)

Serving size: ½ cup (the size of half a tennis ball)

Calories: 145

Sweet and mashed potatoes are a must at any Thanksgiving dinner. Luckily, you won’t consume too many carbs and calories if you only eat half a cup.

Tip: Don’t drown the potatoes in gravy. Doing so not only lessens the flavor, but it also adds unnecessary Thanksgiving calories.

Cornbread (Without Butter)

Serving size: 1 medium muffin or square (the size of a bar of soap)

Calories: 175

A serving of cornbread without butter has around 175 calories. It’s best to limit yourself to one piece of cornbread.

Think about it, this pastry has more calories than a serving of white meat turkey.

Butter or Margarine

Serving size: 1 teaspoon (the size of a dice)

Calories: 36

Adding a teaspoon of butter or margarine on your cornbread will lead to an extra 36 calories.

Pie

Serving size: ⅛ of a 9” pie (the size of a standard light bulb)

Calories: 323 (pumpkin), 456 (pecan)

Thanksgiving day is the perfect opportunity for families to bust out their best pie recipe. However, if you want to cut back on the calories in your Thanksgiving dinner, stick to eating pumpkin pie.

But if you simply can’t resist the goodness of a pecan pie, just try to lessen the amount you’re eating.

Drinks

Let’s face it: no Thanksgiving dinner is complete without a few rounds of drinks. But keep in mind that alcoholic beverages add a ton to your Thanksgiving calories. Plus, they offer zero nutritional value.

However, Thanksgiving only comes once a year. So we can’t blame you for having a glass or two.

Overall, the best pick among these drinks is champagne. Meanwhile, try to steer clear of the sugary cocktail drinks and calorie-filled beer.

White Wine

Calories: 121 per glass

White wine is a great alcoholic drink that helps lower cholesterol and balance your blood sugar. After all the food you ate, a glass of white wine may be the cleanser you need.

Red Wine

Calories: 127 per glass

While it does have 127 calories per glass, red wine contains plenty of antioxidants that are helpful to the body. You can opt to have a glass or two this Thanksgiving.

Champagne

Calories: 78 per glass

Champagne drinks are very smooth and easy to drink. Plus, they only have 78 calories per glass. They’re the ideal alcoholic beverage to down this coming holiday season.

Regular Beer

Calories: 150 per bottle

Many of us are too lazy to do anything other than pop open a bottle of beer. But if you want to stick to your fitness goals, it’s best to lay off the regular beer this Thanksgiving.

Light Beer

Calories: 95 per bottle

If you really don’t want to drink anything other than beer, don’t worry! Opt for light beer instead. It packs as much punch and flavor as regular beer minus the extra calories and carbs.

Generic Cocktail

Calories: 155 per glass

This Thanksgiving, it’s best to skip out on these sugary cocktail drinks. They contain too many sweeteners and juice that add up to carb- and calorie-consumption.

1. Full-Fat Dairy Products

Full-fat dairy products are very high in calories and saturated fat. Be careful when buying products such as cheese, milk, and yogurt.

A good way to get rid of belly fat is to avoid foods with refined carbs, also known as “simple” carbs. They promote inflammation and weight gain. Plus, refined carbs spike your sugar levels.

Replace these foods with whole-grain, fiber-enriched foods instead, also known as “complex” carbs.

3. Fried Foods

One way to lose belly fat naturally is to avoid frying your food using cooking oil.

Due to its high-fat content, it takes much longer to digest these types of food. Plus, excessive amounts can lead to digestive tract complications such as acid reflux and heartburn.

If you’re able to, choose to bake your meals instead of frying them. Switching to olive, coconut, or avocado oil will allow you to increase the amount of polyunsaturated and monounsaturated (healthy) fats in your diet while limiting the amount of saturated and trans fats in your diet.

4. Candy and Soda

These sugar-filled treats are full of empty calories. They offer no nutritional value so the body stores them as fat.

If you’re a fan of diet sodas, you might want to reconsider your choice of drink. It’s no healthier than regular soda and only increases your cravings for sweets.

To satisfy your sweet tooth, drink a glass of fruit-infused water instead. Add some slices of lemons and cucumbers for a cool, refreshing, and healthy alternative to soda and sweets.

5. Alcohol

If you really want to lose stubborn belly fat fast, then you need to avoid alcoholic drinks.

Similar to sodas, they’re filled with empty calories that offer zero nutritional value. Plus, it slows down your body’s fat-burning process.

If you’re going to drink alcohol, choose beverages with lower calories such as wine. Also, remember to drink water in between shots to prevent over-consumption. It’s also a great way to avoid a painful hangover.

6. Salted Snacks

To rid your body of fat, you need to avoid salted snacks such as potato chips. Salt retains water which makes you feel bloated and gain weight.

Choose healthier snacks such as nuts, veggie slices, and whole grain crackers instead.

7. Natural and Artificial Sweeteners

Avoid processed foods with artificial sugars and high-fructose corn syrup. Similarly, fruit juices contain massive amounts of calories that may end up being stored as belly fat.

The best thing to do is to eliminate all processed foods from your diet. Then, instead of drinking fruit drinks, eat the fruits whole and fresh. Also, it’s best to juice vegetables for a refreshing, healthy drink.

One of the best tips for losing belly fat is to steer clear of diet foods. Diet meals and shakes may contain heaps of sodium and preservatives. Also, some protein bars have a higher calorie-content than chocolate bars. If you’re going to consume protein bars, look for protein bars with less than 6 grams of sugar.

One of the best ways to lose belly fat is to make your own diet meals and protein bars instead. Choose all-natural ingredients brimming with vitamins and nutrients.

9. Processed Meat

Processed meats such as bacon, cold cuts, and hotdogs have high levels of saturated fats and calories. Excessive consumption might put you at risk of heart disease and stroke.

Ditch the processed foods and switch to fresh meat instead. But keep in mind that you should also observe moderation when eating fresh meat.

While vegetables are healthy, the dressings that come with pre-packaged salads are high in trans fat and calories. The same goes for sides such as cheese and croutons.

How Many Calories Should I Eat to Lose Weight | FAQs

What Are Calories?

Calories are units of energy measurement. Foods, drinks, supplements, and even condiments all contain calories. We can also measure how much energy we use by counting the calories we burn in a day.

If you want to achieve your fitness goals, you need to be mindful of how many calories you consume in a single day. Remember: to lose weight, the basic rule is to be in caloric deficit – eat fewer calories than you burn.

How Many Calories Should I Eat to Lose Weight?

The amount of calories you need to eat depends on a number of different factors including your age, weight, body, gender, and activity level. That being said, each person has unique individual attributes that are the basis of caloric needs and consumption.

The average female requires approximately 2,000 calories per day, while males generally require around 2,500 calories. With these basic needs in mind, to maintain weight, a female should target a diet consisting of 2000 calories and males should consume 2500 calories.

With respect to weight loss, being in a caloric deficit is the goal. In general, a healthy deficit is 300-500 calories below an individual’s caloric needs. Considering the above information, women should target 1500-1700 calories per day and men should target 2000-2200 calories per day.

How Do I Keep Track Of The Number Of Calories I Consume?

If you want a more accurate number to follow, you can use a calorie calculator. There are plenty of online calculators that can help you determine how much you need to eat to lose weight and maintain lean muscle.

Keep in mind that these are just tools to help you count your calories. They can assist you in creating a fitness plan, but they won’t be your complete diet guide.

How Many Calories Should I Eat to Lose Weight Without Exercise?

While you may be able to shed off a few pounds through calorie-restriction alone, you’ll also be sacrificing lean muscle by doing so. If you want to achieve a lean, fit body, you need to combine proper diet with regular exercise.

How Can I Lose Weight Without Starving Myself?

The best way to still feel full even if you’re cutting back on calories is to avoid junk foods that offer zero nutrients. Chips, cakes, and beer contain empty calories that don’t provide any nutritional value.

If you want to feel full even on a low-calorie diet, opt to eat healthier meals with vitamins and nutrients. Also, replace sugary drinks such as sodas and juices with water, black coffee, or unsweetened tea.

Additionally, proteins and healthy fats help satiate the human body, meaning that consumption of protein-rich and healthy fatty foods will help to keep you fuller for longer periods of time.

What to Eat After a Workout: 7 Meals You Should Try!

 

1. Rice Crackers and Peanut Butter

For a quick, simple post-workout meal, you can opt for some rice crackers and peanut butter. This meal contains enough nutrients to ensure your body’s muscle recovery.

In fact, 100 grams of peanut butter has 25g of protein, 50g of fat, and 20g of carbohydrates. In addition, this meal does not require you to do any preparation. Just bring a jar of peanut butter and some rice crackers to the gym to be eaten after your workout.

2. Protein Shake and Banana

If you can’t decide on what to eat after a workout, then you can stick to the basic protein shake and banana combo. This proven combo is designed specifically for quick nutrient absorption during your anabolic window.

Throw in a scoop or two of your favorite protein powder into a cup, shake it, then have a banana to go with it. In under 10 minutes, you’ll have consumed enough nutrients to promote muscle growth and recovery.

3. Avocado, Toast, and Eggs

Eggs are praised in the fitness industry for their versatility and nutritional value. In fact, 100 grams of eggs have 13g of protein, 11g of fat, and only 155 calories.

Serve these eggs any way you want alongside some avocados and toast and you’ll have a delicious nutrient-packed post-workout meal.

4. Cereal and Milk

Do you know what to eat after a workout that requires virtually no preparation? Your favorite breakfast duo, cereal and milk, can actually be eaten at any time of the day.

For better nutrient consumption, you want to go for a brand that’s rich in protein and fiber but has low sugar content.

5. Greek Yogurt, Berries, and Granola

Greek yogurt has less sugar and more protein than regular yogurt. This makes it an ideal component of your post-workout meal! If you have a cup of Greek yogurt, you don’t have to wonder about what to eat after a workout.

Just throw in some berries and granola in your Greek yogurt, and you’ll have a quick and easy post-workout meal.

6. Grilled Chicken With Roasted Vegetables

If you’re the type who prepares their meals in advance, then one post-workout meal you can try is grilled chicken with roasted vegetables. The chicken gives you the protein and fat you need while the vegetables provide you with minerals.

Grill a few pounds of chicken and veggies and put them in different containers to bring with you to eat after your workout.

7. Cottage Cheese and Fruits

If you ask a random gym-goer what to eat after a workout, they’ll most likely say something that involves either cottage cheese or fruits. Eating cottage cheese and fruits after a workout give you the protein, minerals, and carbohydrates you need for muscle growth. How much protein does it have? 226 grams of cottage cheese has around 28g of protein and only 163 calories.

On the other hand, fruits are packed with healthy and easy-to-digest carbohydrates.
