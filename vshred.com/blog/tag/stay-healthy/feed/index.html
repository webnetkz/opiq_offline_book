You may be an ideal weight.

There isn’t a better time to self-reflect on this… than WORLD HEALTH DAY!

Every year the World Health Organization (WHO) deems April 7th as World Health Day.
A global health awareness day focused on promoting healthy living habits that promote human health and well-being.

When it comes to health … your number one goal should be taking steps to be PROactive instead of REactive.

Hand washing, coughing into your elbow, limiting potential exposure to germs, and other common-sense practices are indeed important… but there are other things you must do to ensure you are healthy and provide support to help keep your immune system strong!

5 PROACTIVE WAYS TO STAY HEALTHY

(1) Get Enough Quality Sleep

We know sleep is important, yet it’s often the first thing we sacrifice to fit everything into our hectic schedules.

Sleep is essential if you want your brain and body to work at full capacity. Not getting enough shut-eye trickles down into every other part of your life.

In terms of immune health, studies show sleep deprivation can impact your immune system, leaving your defenses down and slowing recovery from workouts..

Being exhausted can also affect your ability to lose weight, especially stubborn fat. This is primarily because not getting enough shut-eye has a significant impact on your hormones.

Your stress hormone cortisol goes into overdrive when you don’t get enough sleep. Excess production of cortisol can lead to some unpleasant effects, including weight gain, inefficient immune system function, and digestive issues.

Your “hunger hormones” (leptin and ghrelin) are also thrown off. Leptin decreases hunger and ghrelin stimulates your appetite. If you aren’t getting enough sleep, ghrelin spikes, and leptin plummets. This leaves you hungry (even if you aren’t) and more prone to make unhealthy choices.

You want to INCREASE your leptin levels. The way to do that is… deep sleep.

Sleep quality matters just as much as how long you are sleeping!
Restless sleep is not fat-burning & immune-supporting sleep!!

(2) Exercise Regularly

Something else that will help you sleep better and feel better .. is exercise!

For decades, researchers have investigated how exercise affects the immune system.
Most agree that a little bit of activity every day will help maintain a healthy immune system.

Simply going for a daily walk or jog can be a great first step.

However, to see the most health benefits, it’s important to get in 30 minutes of some sort of physical activity every day. A few days a week, incorporate longer exercise sessions that incorporate strength training.

(3) Follow A Sustainable Nutrient-Rich Diet

Most people see the word diet and picture a plate full of nothing but bland lean protein and green vegetables and believe that is what it takes to be healthy.

You do not need to starve yourself, and you do not need to restrict food groups. Doing these things is the opposite of “healthy” and will almost always backfire!

In terms of fat loss, in most cases, you will need to eat in a caloric deficit to lose body fat.

You can still eat foods you enjoy as long as you prioritize nutrient-rich foods that are not overly processed. Focus on eating a variety of fruits and vegetables, lean proteins, whole grains, and healthy fats.

(4) Focus On Mental Health

The World Health Organization defines health as… “a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being.”

Take a moment today to ask yourself how you are feeling MENTALLY!

A healthy body is not going to do you much good if you do not have control of your mental health.

In fact, it may be impossible.

Mental health and physical health are very connected. If you aren’t in a good headspace, you will be less likely to start (and then stick) with healthy behaviors.

On the flip side, when you aren’t physically healthy or have negative body-image issues… this can have a serious impact on your mental health.

Don’t wait until you are in “crisis mode” to make mental health a priority. Start forming healthy mental habits today that will help you cope better with stress that pops up in the future.

Here are a few things that may help you out….

  • Talk To Someone
    Seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness. Whether it is a professional or a trusted friend, do not keep your emotions locked up inside.
  • Be Positive
    Negative thoughts will quickly put you in a bitter mood. Researchers found that long term pessimists had an almost 20 percent higher risk of dying than those who were optimistic! No matter how bleak things appear, there is ALWAYS a silver lining. Seek the positive and try your best to focus on that!
  • Find Things To Be Grateful For
    It’s no surprise that researchers found that people who focused on gratitude (even as little as once a week) were happier and had fewer physical complaints! One study even found that expressing gratitude may have LONG lasting effects on the brain. Speak things you are grateful for, write a gratitude letter, or keep a gratitude journal!
  • Help Others
    We are hardwired to love! Doing something kind for someone else will leave you with “feel good” emotions that will positively impact your own self-esteem!
  • Improve Your Life Outlook
    Try meditating, mindfulness, positive affirmations, and/or prayer. These practices can provide instant stress relief. Over time, they can help make positive thinking more automatic.

(5) Prioritize Gut Health

If your gut health isn’t optimal, your immune system can’t be either. 70-80% of the cells that make up your immune system reside in your gut wall!

Immune support is only one reason you need to place a high emphasis on gut health.

Did you know your gut is the only organ that has it’s very own nervous system?! It has more neurotransmitters than your brain! A healthy gut is crucial for optimal mental health and brain function.

Your gut also houses a second set of genes (that many call the second genome) that you probably didn’t even realize you have! These are not the same genetics passed down from your parents. You have the ability to change these genes. But like the genes you can’t change, your second genome can impact the way your body looks, feels, and functions.

Inside your gut is over three pounds of bacteria… known as your gut microbiome.
Many things can cause unhealthy bacteria to populate and take over.

  • Stress

  • Environmental Toxins
