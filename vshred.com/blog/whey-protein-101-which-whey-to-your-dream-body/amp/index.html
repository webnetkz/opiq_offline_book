Which Whey to Your Dream Body?

Whey protein has long been a go-to for anyone looking to improve their body… and with good reason. Refueling your body with a high-quality protein post-workout is the fastest, easiest way to avoid falling into a catabolic (muscle wasting) state after training.

Whey acts fast to get into your system and begin repairing damaged muscle cells while building new muscle fibers.

The run-off product from cheese and yogurt making, whey was once discarded as useless… Until research scientists stumbled upon the fact that whey does some amazing things in your body.

Whey has helped people:

  • Burn fat faster
  • Maintain muscle while dieting
  • Improve strength
  • Increase lean, metabolism-boosting muscle mass
  • Boost immune function
  • So, why whey and not soy, meat, or vegetable proteins?

Basically, your body absorbs whey proteins better than any other source. Whey scores the highest on the Biological Value (BV) scale. This means you absorb more protein per serving with whey then you do with any other protein source.

IF… you consume the right type of whey protein.

Sugary Floor Sweepings AKA Whey Protein Concentrate

All those low-cost protein powders lining the shelves of big-box stores like Walmart, Costco, and Target are, more than likely, jam-packed with whey protein concentrate (WPC).

It sounds good…After all, whey is good. And protein concentrate sounds like super strong, concentrated proteins for your body. But here’s the problem…

Those protein powders are really cheap because the whey inside is, for the most part, nothing more than sweetened floor sweepings.

Whey Protein Concentrate (WPC) is a low-cost, low-quality form of whey that finds its way into cheap powders and protein bars. The problem with WPC? It has an extremely low bioavailability score. This means your body only digests, and is able to use, a small percentage of the protein.

The rest is simply passed off as waste.

Not only that, but WPC actually has fewer protein grams per serving than superior forms of whey that work with your body to burn fat and build muscle. For example, some WPC products can contain as low as 34% protein per serving. That means, each scoop contains only 34% protein, the rest is carbs, fat, ash, and lactose.

Think of it this way… that cheap 3lb container of protein isn’t so cheap when you consider that as little as 34% of it is actually protein. That means over 2lbs of that powder is NOT protein….pretty scary right?
