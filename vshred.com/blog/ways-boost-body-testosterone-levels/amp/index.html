One of the toughest things men have to deal with as they age is the declining levels of testosterone. Just how important is testosterone? Testosterone is the male hormone responsible for muscle mass and fast metabolism. Read on to learn how to boost testosterone.

How To Boost Your Body’s Testosterone Levels | Get It Up

By the time men hit 30, testosterone levels start to decline. This puts them at greater risk to different conditions — from cardiovascular problems to type 2 diabetes and obesity.

Testosterone is important for women, as well. Testosterone levels among women also peak during their 20s.

Women have traces of the anabolic hormone, though it is only around 10% of what men usually have. Women who lose their body’s testosterone also experience the same problems such as weight gain and sluggish metabolism.

Fortunately, there are ways to boost your testosterone levels. Here are simple things you can implement to boost your body’s testosterone.

 

1. Eat the Right Type of Dietary Fat

Dietary fat is considered by many as a bad thing. However, let’s agree that there are different types of fats you can consume.

Fats are needed by the body for metabolizing vitamins A, D, E and K. According to studies, fats can help boost the body’s testosterone level.

Consuming controlled levels of quality fats such as coconut oil, egg yolk, and olive oil can make a huge difference to how much testosterone your body produces.

2. Choose the Right Type of Cholesterol

Testosterone is basically made from cholesterol. Therefore, there’s a strong relationship between levels of testosterone to the amount of cholesterol you take.

But to stay healthy, you have to consider the type of cholesterol you are consuming. According to experts, HDL or high-density lipoprotein is the type of cholesterol you want to incorporate into your meals.

It is associated with the better cardiovascular condition and weight loss. What types of food usually contain HDL? Fish, vegetables, and fruits are among the most popular options for HDL-rich meals.

4. Be Smart in Your Training

If you intend to workout, try sticking to programs that have higher volumes, rather than low-rep-high intensity workouts.

There is enough evidence stating 70 to 80 percent of your 10-rep max repeatedly done could elicit the best testosterone response.

Also, focus on larger muscle groups of the body. Free weights have been proven to increase testosterone levels the most. If you are the type of person who loves to run for hours, you may want to stop this.

Longer hours of training could decrease the amount of testosterone in your body. As rule of thumb, stick to a 60-minute moderate to high-intensity workout instead.

5. Get Enough Sleep

Sleep is just as important as working out. Lack of sleep dramatically reduces your testosterone levels which then triggers body fat formation.

To get the most testosterone from sleeping, you should sleep for around 7 to 9 hours in a day.
